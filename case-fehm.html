The Challenge

A Beautiful Brand With a Website That Wasn't Selling

The Fehm by SMW is a Pakistani luxury jewelry brand with strong product quality and an engaged Instagram following. But their website wasn't converting. The homepage copy was generic, the layout wasn't optimized for their audience's buying behavior, and there was no emotional narrative connecting the brand to Pakistani women who were their ideal buyers.

The product catalog was also incomplete and inconsistently formatted — making it hard for visitors to browse and buy. The brand needed a full website overhaul that spoke directly to their audience with the right tone, emotional triggers, and product presentation.

My Role

Website Analyst + Copywriter + GHL Developer

I handled every layer of the project — from the initial website audit to copy strategy, product catalog build, and two full homepage versions coded in HTML/CSS for deployment inside GoHighLevel. Each section was built as a modular block with unique IDs so individual sections could be reused independently.

The Process

Audit → Strategy → Build

Step 01
Full Website Audit
Conducted a comprehensive audit of the existing site — analyzing homepage structure, copy tone, product page layout, mobile experience, CTA placement, and conversion gaps.
Step 02
Audience & Copy Strategy
Developed a copy strategy tailored to Pakistani women — emotional storytelling, cultural resonance, aspiration-driven language, and product benefit framing that matched local buying psychology.
Step 03
Homepage Copy Rewrite
Rewrote the full homepage copy in a persuasive tone — hero headline, brand story section, product highlights, testimonial framing, and CTA language — all aligned with the target audience.
Step 04
Product Catalog Build
Built a complete product catalog from scratch — consistent naming, pricing display, product description format, and category structure that made browsing intuitive and purchase decisions easier.
Step 05
Two HTML Homepage Versions
Coded two full homepage versions in HTML/CSS — Version A (elegant and minimal) and Version B (bold and editorial). Both built with section-level IDs so individual blocks could be lifted and reused inside GHL independently.
