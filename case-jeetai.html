The Challenge

Selling AI to Skeptical B2B Decision-Makers

Jeet AI Clinic offers AI automation services to business owners and enterprise clients. The core challenge: their target audience — B2B decision-makers — are deeply skeptical of AI claims. They've heard "AI will save you time and money" so many times it's become noise. A generic AI agency website wasn't going to work.

They needed a website that felt credible, technical, and specific — one that spoke to real business outcomes, overcame objections before they were raised, and positioned the agency as a trusted implementation partner, not another chatbot reseller.

My Role

GHL Website Builder + B2B Conversion Copywriter

I built the entire website inside GoHighLevel using custom HTML and CSS injected directly into GHL sections — giving the site a premium, coded feel while staying inside the client's existing GHL ecosystem. I also wrote all copy — structured around objection handling, outcome-first messaging, and specific AI use cases relevant to their target industries.

The Process

Design & Build Breakdown

Step 01
Audience & Objection Research
Mapped the typical B2B buyer journey for AI services — identifying the 5 core objections: "it's too expensive," "it won't work for my industry," "it's too complex," "I don't trust AI," and "I've tried this before."
Step 02
Copy Architecture
Structured the page to lead with outcome proof, then address objections mid-page, then show process and credibility, then close with a low-friction CTA (free audit vs. "buy now"). Every section had one job.
Step 03
GHL Custom Build
Built the full website inside GoHighLevel using custom HTML/CSS in GHL's code blocks — dark theme, animated sections, responsive grid layouts, and a professional visual identity that matched the premium positioning.
Step 04
Mobile Optimization
Tested and refined all sections for mobile — adjusting font sizes, stacking layouts, and ensuring CTAs were thumb-accessible. GHL's default mobile view was overridden in several sections using CSS media queries.
